# Chiri.AI: Week 1 X (Twitter) Content Strategy
**Inspired by Wade Foster's Playbook & Chiri's Ontology Story**

## 1. Analysis of Wade Foster's X Strategy
Wade Foster (CEO of Zapier) has a distinct, highly effective style on X that perfectly aligns with Chiri's goals. Here is what works for him:

*   **Show, Don't Tell (Internal Dogfooding):** His pinned tweet is about how Zapier uses their own new product internally (">1,000,000 actions executed so far"). This builds massive credibility.
*   **Bold, Definitive Statements:** "We’re setting a new standard at Zapier. 100% of new hires must be fluent in AI." This creates a polarizing, shareable moment.
*   **Actionable Value:** He shares job descriptions ("Steal this job description") and specific use cases.
*   **Milestone Celebrations:** He celebrates company milestones (e.g., "We now have more AI agents than employees") in a way that highlights the product's value.
*   **Tone:** Direct, confident, slightly technical, but focused on business outcomes. He speaks like an operator, not a marketer.

## 2. Synthesizing with Chiri's Talking Points
Chiri's core narrative is about being the "operating system for the modern organization" and the "metaverse for work." The key differentiator is **Ontology** (mapping the organization's digital DNA) and **Zero Disruption** (integrating into Slack, Teams, etc., rather than forcing a new UI).

We will blend Wade Foster's definitive, operator-led tone with Chiri's enterprise-grade talking points to create a compelling first week of content.

## 3. Week 1 Content Calendar

### Day 1: The "Internal Dogfooding" Hook (Launch Day)
*Goal: Establish credibility by showing how Chiri uses Chiri, mirroring Wade Foster's pinned tweet strategy.*

**Tweet:**
> Today we are opening early access to Chiri.AI.
> 
> We have been running our entire company on it for the last six months. Our digital workers have executed over 500,000 tasks directly inside our Slack and Microsoft Teams environments.
> 
> We are letting a small group of forward-thinking enterprise teams try it first.
> 
> Who we are looking for: Teams that want to augment their human workforce without forcing them to learn another software platform.
> 
> Apply here: [Link]

**Media:** A short, polished video showing a Chiri digital worker completing a complex task inside a standard Slack channel.

### Day 2: The Bold Standard (The "Wade Foster" Play)
*Goal: Make a definitive statement about the future of work that sparks debate and sharing.*

**Tweet:**
> We are setting a new standard for enterprise software.
> 
> If your new AI tool requires your employees to log into a separate dashboard, it is already obsolete.
> 
> The future of work is not humans learning how to behave like machines. It is AI integrating seamlessly into the workflows humans already use.
> 
> Chiri lives where your team lives: Slack, Teams, Email, and your CRM. No new UI required.

### Day 3: The Technical Deep Dive (Ontology)
*Goal: Establish the "nerdy/strong voice" and explain the technical moat (Ontology) in accessible terms.*

**Thread:**
> **Tweet 1:** Most enterprise AI deployments fail because they treat data as isolated silos. They miss the context. 🧵
> 
> **Tweet 2:** Inside your company, valuable information is fragmented across emails, Slack threads, and databases. To an LLM, this is just noise.
> 
> **Tweet 3:** This is why we built Chiri on the concept of Ontology. Ontology is the mapping of your organization's knowledge, relationships, and operational intelligence. It is your digital DNA.
> 
> **Tweet 4:** Chiri does not just read your data. It understands the relationships between your systems and the patterns in your operations. It knows *how* your company works.
> 
> **Tweet 5:** This allows our digital workers to execute complex, multi-step workflows with the same context as a human employee. This is the difference between a chatbot and an operating system.

### Day 4: The Milestone / Vision
*Goal: Share a compelling vision of the "Blended Workforce."*

**Tweet:**
> Interesting milestone at one of our early enterprise design partners: They now have more digital workers executing background tasks than human employees doing manual data entry.
> 
> The result? Their human team is spending 100% of their time on strategy, judgment, and relationship management.
> 
> This is what the convergence of human and digital workers looks like.

### Day 5: Actionable Value (The "Steal This" Play)
*Goal: Provide immediate, practical value to the audience.*

**Tweet:**
> The hardest part of deploying AI is figuring out where to start.
> 
> Steal our internal framework for identifying which workflows to hand over to digital workers.
> 
> Look for tasks that are:
> 1. High volume
> 2. Rule-based
> 3. Require accessing multiple systems (e.g., pulling from Salesforce to update a Slack channel)
> 
> Stop wasting human potential on robotic tasks. Let Chiri handle the execution.

## 4. Key Formatting Rules Applied
*   **No Em-Dashes:** Replaced all em-dashes with standard punctuation or line breaks as per the style guide.
*   **Executive Tone:** The language is peer-to-peer, authoritative, and focused on business outcomes.
*   **Enterprise Context:** Every post is grounded in a universal workplace issue (e.g., software fatigue, data silos, manual data entry).
*   **No Apologetic Language:** The tone is confident and direct. There are no phrases like "I'm not looking to pitch anything."
