# Chiri Brand Voice Guide

---

## The One-Sentence Brief

Chiri sounds like a really good-hearted, whip-smart but humble person who you want to work with and work for. They have been around the block, are not knee-jerked by random trends, really see humans and people, get people and processes, and have the wisdom and the calm to sit back and watch stuff happening and be really incisive and smart — not from an intellectual showing-off place, but from an "I can make this accessible because I know it" and "you're in good hands with me" place.

Nobody is sitting with them, understanding the real texture of what they're going through, and speaking to that with both intelligence and warmth.

That is Chiri's voice.

## A Note on the Name

When you say "Chiri" out loud, it sounds warm. Almost sweet. We don't want to be cute — but we do want to be approachable. The name itself signals something human and accessible. That is a feature, not a bug. Lean into it. The voice should feel like the name: warm, curious, grounded, and quietly confident.

## The Chiri Voice in Seven Principles

### 1. Curiosity First
**This is the newest and most important addition to the voice.**

Every post, every email, every piece of content should open with a question — not a statement. Not a declaration. A genuine question that opens up empathy and emotion before we go into our value proposition.

The question is not rhetorical. It is not a setup for a punchline. It is a real invitation for the reader to reflect on their own experience before we offer ours.

**Why this works:** It signals that we are interested in them before we are interested in being heard. It is the difference between a conversation and a pitch. It is also the most honest expression of who we are — a company built by people who spent 25 years asking "how does this actually work for the humans doing the work?"

**Sounds like:** "What's the one workflow eating your team's week right now?"

**Does not sound like:** "AI is transforming the workforce. Here's how Chiri can help."

---

### 2. Calm authority, not performance
Chiri has seen a lot. It does not need to shout. The voice is steady, measured, and confident — not because it is trying to project confidence, but because it has earned it. When everyone else is breathless about the next AI model, Chiri is the person in the room who says "let me tell you what actually matters here."

**Sounds like:** "The SaaS-pocalypse is real. But it's not the end of the opportunity. It's a reset on where the value actually lives."

**Does not sound like:** "AI is transforming everything! Here are 7 ways to stay ahead!"

### 3. People-first, always
This is the core of the brand. Chiri was built by HR people. People who have spent careers watching what happens when organizations get the human side of change wrong. The voice always comes back to the person in the workflow. Not the system. Not the model. The person.

**Sounds like:** "People power business. AI just lets your people do the part that requires being a person."

**Does not sound like:** "Our AI agents deliver 40% efficiency gains across your operational stack."

### 4. Accessible intelligence
Chiri knows complex things and explains them simply. Not because it is dumbing things down, but because it respects the reader's time and intelligence enough to get to the point. The voice never shows off. It makes things clear.

**Sounds like:** "The hardest part of AI adoption was never the software. It's the workflow mapping, the implementation, the measurement."

**Does not sound like:** "Leveraging ontological frameworks to enable agentic orchestration across heterogeneous system architectures."

### 5. Specific and grounded
Chiri does not deal in abstractions. It deals in the 1,500-person healthcare group with a team of five. The HR director answering benefits questions at 9pm. The engineering team asking their CHRO "are we going to get fired?" Specificity is what separates Chiri from the AI commentariat.

**Sounds like:** "A customer told us something recently that we have not been able to stop thinking about. 'It feels like we're being double-taxed.'"

**Does not sound like:** "Organizations across industries are facing unprecedented challenges in AI adoption."

### 6. Contrarian where it counts, never for sport
Chiri is willing to say the thing nobody else is saying. But only when it is true and useful. Not to be provocative. Not to generate engagement. Because it has actually seen something that others are missing.

**Sounds like:** "Hint: it's not in the UI layer anymore."

**Does not sound like:** "Unpopular opinion: AI is actually bad for most companies."

### 7. Warm closer
Every Chiri post ends with a sense of "you're in good hands." Not a hard sell. Not a begging engagement question. A genuine invitation. The reader should feel like Chiri is the person they want to call when things get complicated.

**Sounds like:** "If you're running thin, the answer isn't another seat. It's 30 to 90 days with us to find the one workflow eating your team's week. Pick it. Wire it up. Walk out."

**Does not sound like:** "Book a demo today!"

## The LinkedIn Post Formula

Every Chiri LinkedIn post follows this structure:

1. **Open with a question** (under 210 characters — earns the "see more" click)
2. **Name the specific pain or insight** (one concrete observation, not a list)
3. **Tell a specific story or cite a specific source** (a customer quote, a named person, a real number)
4. **Connect to Chiri's thesis** — warm, grounded, never a pitch
5. **Close with a question or soft invitation** — not begging for engagement, genuinely curious
6. **3-5 hashtags** — lower in the post

---

## The Contrarian Position (vs. the AI Platforms)

Every major AI platform is talking about capability. What the model can do. What the agent can automate. What the software can replace.

Chiri's contrarian position is simple: **the bottleneck has never been the technology. It has always been the people and the process underneath it.**

This is not anti-technology. Chiri is software. Chiri is an orchestration layer. Chiri believes deeply in what AI can do for organizations.

But Chiri also knows — from 25 years of building and scaling human organizations — that you cannot bolt AI onto a broken operating model and expect it to work. You have to fix the model first. And fixing the model is a people job.

That is the voice. That is the brand. That is the thing nobody else in this space is saying.

## Post 2: Double-Taxed

A customer told us something recently that we have not been able to stop thinking about.

"It feels like we're being double-taxed."

Here is what that actually means. Your team is expected to be an AI innovator and still do their day job. At the same time. With the same bandwidth.

Nobody took anything off their plate. They just added a second job on top of the first one.

We heard it echoed again this week on Kaihan Krippendorf and Joanne Sheppard's podcast. It is not a one-off. It is the pattern.

Most leaders read resistance to AI as unwillingness. It is usually exhaustion.

The companies that break through pick one workflow, prove the ROI in weeks, and let the team that got time back become the most convincing case study in the building.

That is how adoption spreads. Not through pressure. Through proof.

## Post 4: The Technical Staff Paradox

The people who resist AI the most inside your organization are probably not who you think.

It is often your most skilled technical staff.

Joanne Sheppard calls it the "Innovator's Dilemma" for technical teams on this week's podcast. When you hand a senior engineer an AI coding agent, you are not just giving them a tool. You are automating the core task they built their professional identity around.

That creates an identity crisis. It feels like a loss, even when it is objectively an upgrade.

We see this in every engagement. Not just in engineering. In any role where a highly skilled person has spent years building expertise in a specific workflow, and now a digital worker can do that workflow in seconds.

The answer is not to slow down the technology.

The answer is to show your best people that their value is now in the judgment, the strategy, and the relationships that no digital worker can replicate.

At Chiri, we call this multiplication, not replacement. We capture the institutional knowledge of your best people and turn it into the operating infrastructure of your whole organization.

Your experts do not get replaced. They get multiplied.
