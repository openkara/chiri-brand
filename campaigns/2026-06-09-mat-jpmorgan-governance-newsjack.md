## Variant A — LinkedIn

What's actually stopping you from letting an AI agent run on its own for a week?

For most teams I talk to, the honest answer comes down to trust.

JPMorgan put it on the record this week. Their Chief Analytics Officer, Derek Waldron, said the long-running agents they want to deploy this year, the ones that run for hours, then days, then weeks, still aren't ready for corporate use. The holdup he named was security.

We've spent the last two years building for exactly that holdup. Our CTO, Matthew Wimberly, puts it plainly: he treats an agent the same way he treats an employee, and he makes sure it can't touch anything it shouldn't. Every action it takes is traceable. Who it acted for, what it accessed, when, and what it did.

That's the layer Chiri exists to be. We close the whole loop. We map how the work actually happens inside your company, build the agent around it, then run it with access controls, cost controls, full traceability, and a person kept in the loop for the calls that need one.

Here's why that matters for the people doing the work. JPMorgan also said AI could eventually let a banker cover 50% more clients. A person only hands off that much once they trust what's running underneath them. Governance is what earns that trust.

So if your team is sitting on agents nobody quite trusts to run alone yet, you're asking exactly the right question. And there's a real answer to it.

What would your best people do with their week if they trusted the work to run without them?
