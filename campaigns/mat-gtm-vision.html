The Question Every CEO Is Getting Wrong

Everyone is asking what AI tools they need.
The right question is:
how do we transform our organization
to think elastically?

The Static Organization
How most enterprises are built today
  • Fixed org charts. Roles defined by title, not capability.
  • Institutional knowledge lives in people's heads. When they leave, it leaves.
  • AI is bolted on top. The dysfunction underneath accelerates.
  • People fear replacement. They hide their AI use. Adoption stalls.
  • Transformation is a project. It ends. The org goes back to static.
The Elastic Enterprise
What the companies that win are building now
  • Roles expand and contract with the work. Humans own judgment. AI owns execution.
  • Institutional knowledge is captured, structured, and deployed as an asset.
  • AI is built into the operating system. It learns the org. The org learns from it.
  • People are elevated. Their expertise trains the agents. They become multiplied.
  • Transformation is permanent. The org self-optimizes. Change is the default state.

AI is not a technology revolution. It is a people operations revolution. Every company racing to deploy AI tools is asking the wrong question. The tools are not the constraint. The organization is. And the only person who can solve an organizational problem is someone who has spent their career building organizations. That is what Mat Caldwell has done for 25 years.

Chapter 01 · The Keynote Arc
A skeleton of the keynote Mat could give

Click through to see how the story unfolds — from the problem every leader recognizes to the elastic future they need to build.

Slide 01 · The Opening
"I made the CEO of Instacart
deliver groceries."
I open with the story. Not a statistic. Not a framework. A moment every leader in the room immediately understands. The CEO, on a bike, delivering groceries in San Francisco. Why? Because you cannot build a system for people you have never been. The room laughs. Then goes quiet.
Opens with a human story, not a slide deck. Sets up that this talk comes from someone who has been in the room, not just studied it.
Slide 02 · The Problem
"80% of AI transformations fail.
Not because the technology is wrong."
The data lands. Then I reframe it. The technology is not the problem. The organization is. Static org charts. Institutional knowledge locked in people's heads. Roles that cannot flex. AI does not create these problems. It reveals them. Fast. And at scale.
Sharp and diagnostic. The audience starts nodding. They have seen this. They may be living it.
Slide 03 · The Contrast
"Static. Versus. Elastic."
Two columns. The static organization: fixed roles, siloed knowledge, AI bolted on top. The elastic enterprise: roles that flex with the work, knowledge captured as an asset, humans elevated by the agents they train. Every person in the room knows which column their company lives in right now.
Visual and declarative. This is the slide that gets photographed and posted to LinkedIn during the talk.
Slide 04 · The Human Upside
"People do not lose in
the Elastic Enterprise. They multiply."
This is the turn. The fear in every room is replacement. In an elastic org, your best people do not get replaced by AI. Their expertise trains the agents. Their judgment governs the system. They become multiplied. The people who win are the ones who teach the machine what they know.
Reassuring but honest. This is the moment the CHRO in the front row leans forward.
Slide 05 · The Proof
"I have built this.
At Instacart. At Mozilla. At RocketPower."
Employee 51 at Instacart, scaled to thousands. VP of People at Mozilla through a full organizational transformation. RocketPower built from scratch, 800% growth in 14 months, sold to Kelly Services. Every time, the same principle: get the people system right first, and everything else scales on top of it.
Specific names, numbers, outcomes. No vague claims. The audience trusts him because he has the receipts.
Slide 06 · The Platform
"Chiri is the operating skin
for the Elastic Enterprise."
Not a pitch. A natural conclusion. After 25 minutes building the thesis, Chiri is the answer to the question the audience is now asking: how do we actually build this? ChiriBrain sits on top of every tool, every workflow, every team. It captures institutional knowledge. It builds agents from your best people's expertise.
Earned. The audience is ready for the product because Mat has spent 25 minutes making them feel the problem.
Slide 07 · The Close
"Are we building something static,
or something elastic?"
I do not end with a product demo. I end with a question. The category is forming right now. The companies that move first will define what the Elastic Enterprise looks like. The audience leaves with one question ringing in their head. And they know exactly who to call.
Ends on a question, not an answer. The best talks leave the audience with something to do.
